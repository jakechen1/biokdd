BIOKDD, 2001

1st Workshop on Data Mining in Bioinformatics

August 26, 2001
San Francisco, California, USA
in conjunction with
7th ACM SIGKDD International Conference
Proceedings of the 1st ACM SIGKDD Workshop on Data Mining in Bioinformatics — edited by Mohammed J. Zaki, Jason T. L. Wang, Hannu T. T. Toivonen.
Theme: Founding edition — open call on biological data mining · ~100 participants

Program — Accepted Papers

11 contributed papers (out of 19 submissions, ~58% acceptance) · 2 invited talks · 4 sessions

Microarray Expression Mining

  1. Extracting Knowledge from Gene Expression Data: A Case Study of Batten Disease — Simon M. Lin, Sumeer Dhar, Rose-Mary Boustany (Duke Medical Center)
  2. Mining Microarray Expression Data for Classifier Gene-Cores — Goutham Kurra, Wen Niu, Raj Bhatnagar (U Cincinnati)
  3. Classification of Genes Using Probabilistic Models of Microarray Expression Profiles — Paul Pavlidis, Christopher Tang, William S. Noble (Columbia)
  4. Analysis of an associative memory neural network for pattern identification in gene expression data — Silvio Bicciato, Carlo Di Bello (U Padova) with M. Pandin & G. Didoné (Cittadella Hospital, Italy)

Sequence Assembly

  1. A Learning Algorithm for String Assembly — Mark K. Goldberg, Darren T. Lim, Malik Magdon-Ismail (RPI)
  2. A Probabilistic Approach to Sequence Assembly Validation — Sun Kim (Indiana U), Li Liao, Jean-Francois Tomb (DuPont)

Protein Structure & Classification

  1. Learning to Recognize Brain Specific Proteins Based on Low-level Features from On-line Prediction Servers — Mikael Huss, Henrik Boström, Lars Asker, Joakim Cöster (Virtual Genetics Lab)
  2. Bagging-Like Effects for Decision Trees and Neural Nets in Protein Secondary Structure Prediction — Nitesh Chawla, Thomas E. Moore Jr., Kevin W. Bowyer, Lawrence O. Hall (USF) with Clayton Springer, Philip Kegelmeyer (Sandia)

Sequence Modeling & Clustering

  1. Maximum entropy methods for biological sequence modeling — Eugen C. Buehler, Lyle H. Ungar (U Pennsylvania)
  2. Hierarchical cluster analysis of SAGE data for cancer profiling — Raymond T. Ng, Monica C. Sleumer (UBC), Jörg Sander (U Alberta)
  3. A scalable algorithm for clustering protein sequences — Valerie Guralnik, George Karypis (U Minnesota)

Publication
