Program — Accepted Papers

10 contributed papers (out of 16 submissions, ~63% acceptance) · 1 invited talk · 3 sessions

Structure Mining

  1. Mining Protein Contact Maps — Jingjing Hu, Xiaolan Shen, Yu Shao, Chris Bystroff, Mohammed Zaki (RPI)
  2. Automated Approaches for Classifying Structures — Mukund Deshpande, Michihiro Kuramochi, George Karypis (U Minnesota)
  3. Clustering and Classifying Enzymes in Metabolic Pathways — Li Liao, Jean-Francois Tomb (DuPont), Sen Zhang, Jason Wang (NJIT)
  4. Generalization Methods in Bioinformatics — Steven Eschrich, Nitesh Chawla, Larry Hall (USF)

Data Mining & Drug Design

  1. Incremental Machine Learning to Reduce Biochemistry Lab Costs in the Search for Drug Discovery — George Forman (HP Labs)
  2. Gram-Schmidt Kernels Applied to Structure Activity Analysis for Drug Design — Huma Lodhi, Yike Guo (Imperial College London)
  3. Closed Set Mining of Biological Data — John L. Pfaltz, Christopher M. Taylor (U Virginia)

Gene Expression

  1. E-CAST: A Data Mining Algorithm for Gene Expression Data — Abdelghani Bellaachia, David Portnoy (George Washington U) with Yidong Chen, Abdel G. Elkahloun (NIH)
  2. Visualized Classification of Multiple Sample Types — Li Zhang, Aidong Zhang, Murali Ramanathan (SUNY Buffalo)
  3. GEA: A Toolkit for Gene Expression Analysis — Jessica M. Phan, Raymond Ng, Man Saint Yuen (UBC), Steve Jones (BC Genome Sequence Center)
