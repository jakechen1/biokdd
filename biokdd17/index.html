Held with ACM SIGKDD 2017 in Halifax — bringing together data-mining and biomedical-informatics researchers around deep-learning methods for heterogeneous biological data.

Welcome to BIOKDD 2017

The 16th International Workshop on Data Mining in Bioinformatics encourages KDD researchers to take on the numerous challenges that Bioinformatics offers. This year's edition features the theme Multiscale and Multimodal Analysis for Computational Biology — applying data mining and machine learning to the large amounts of heterogeneous, complex biological and medical data the post-genomic era is producing.

Deep-learning methods are particularly encouraged. The goal is to build accurate predictive and descriptive models from heterogeneous data that enable novel discoveries in basic biology and medicine.

Topics of interest

Deep learning for biological and clinical data Predictive models for complex phenotypes Biological networks & pathways Next-generation sequencing Biomarker & mutation discovery Genotype–phenotype associations Multi-omics integration Comparative genomics Metagenomics RNA-seq & microarray analysis Non-coding RNAs Regulatory motif discovery Structural bioinformatics NGS + proteomics correlation Functional annotation Cheminformatics Biomedical data management Visualization of biological data Semantic web & ontology-driven integration Privacy in genomic data mining

Submission and publication

Papers are at most 9 pages long, single-spaced, font size 10 or larger, with one-inch margins. Camera-ready papers follow the official ACM Proceedings Format. Selected high-quality papers from the workshop are invited to a special issue of IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics (TCBB), continuing the BIOKDD tradition.

Submissions are handled through EasyChair: easychair.org/conferences/?conf=biokdd2017.

Workshop history

This is the 16th edition of BIOKDD. Past workshops attracted 50–100 participants drawn from academia, industry and government labs across the world, with 10–11 contributed papers and 1–2 invited talks each year. Information on past workshops is on the Past Workshops page.
